Core Accounting Equations

Assets = Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity
The fundamental accounting equation — basis of every balance sheet
Net Income = Revenues - Expenses
Shown on the income statement; covers a period of time
RE(end) = RE(beg) + Net Income - Dividends
Retained Earnings — links income statement to the balance sheet

Multi-Step Income Statement Subtotals

Gross Profit = Sales Revenue - COGS
First subtotal in a multi-step income statement
Operating Income = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
Profit from core business operations
Pre-tax Income = Operating Income + Nonoperating Income
Includes investing and financing-related items
Net Income = Pre-tax Income - Income Tax Expense
The "bottom line" — also called earnings or profit

Inventory & COGS

COGS = Beg. Inventory + Purchases - End. Inventory
Rearrange to solve for any missing variable
Weighted Avg. Cost/unit = Total Cost Available / Total Units Available
Used under the weighted average cost flow assumption
Rising prices: LIFO = higher COGS, lower ending inventory
FIFO = lower COGS, higher ending inventory — opposite in falling prices

PP&E & Depreciation

Annual Depreciation = (Cost - Residual Value) / Service Life
Straight-line method — same expense each full year
Partial Year = Annual Depreciation x (Months Used / 12)
When asset is purchased partway through the year
Book Value = Cost - Accumulated Depreciation
Net carrying value shown on the balance sheet

Receivables & Interest

Net AR = Accounts Receivable - Allowance for Uncollectible Accounts
Net realizable value — the amount the company expects to collect
Interest = Principal x Annual Rate x (Time / 12)
Time in months; use /365 if given days

Stockholders' Equity

APIC = Cash Received - (Shares Issued x Par Value)
Additional Paid-in Capital — the "plug" for par value stock issuances
Shares Outstanding = Shares Issued - Treasury Shares
Only outstanding shares receive dividends
Dividends = Shares Outstanding x Dividend Per Share
Used to calculate total dividends declared or paid

Normal Balances — DEALOR Rule

DEBIT balance (increase with debit)

  • Dividends
  • Expenses
  • Assets

CREDIT balance (increase with credit)

  • Liabilities
  • Owners' (Stockholders') Equity
  • Revenues
